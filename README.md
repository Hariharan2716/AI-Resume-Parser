# AI-Resume-Parser

Simple parsing and better word formatting can significantly improve your chances of getting shortlisted.

## Situation-Based Explanation

Imagine two candidates applying for the same Data Analyst role:

- **Candidate A** writes:  
  *"Worked on dashboards, did reports, handled data."*
- **Candidate B** writes:  
  *"Built Power BI dashboards, automated weekly KPI reports, and cleaned 50K+ records using SQL and Python."*

Most companies first use an **Applicant Tracking System (ATS)** to parse resumes before a human recruiter reads them.  
The ATS looks for job-relevant keywords (like **SQL**, **Python**, **Power BI**, **KPI**, **automation**) and checks whether the content is clearly structured.

When words are parsed and placed in the right format:

1. Skills are matched accurately with job requirements.
2. Recruiters quickly understand impact from action-based lines.
3. The resume gets a higher relevance score in ATS filtering.

So, the right parsing of words plus proper formatting can directly increase shortlist probability in company recruitment.
